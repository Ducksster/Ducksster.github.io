NKU Game Jam

A game jam is an event where developers, including programmers, artists, musicians, and others, collaborate to create a game. The twist is that there is a theme that must be followed, and teams only have 48 hours to complete the game. I have, for a long time, wanted to have a game jam at NKU. Unfortunately, I learned that NKU didn't hold any regular game jams. When I learned this, I decided to set out to bring one to campus, and fortunately, I found some allies in this pursuit. A professor and a fellow student had already started talking about bringing a game jam to campus. We worked together to bring this to campus. I reached out to professors to judge and had a poster made by my friend, Areli Lyles. Furthermore, I spread the word to every CS major I could. Both of the people I was working with were in DFX, so my spreading the word to CS majors was very valuable. On the day of the game jam, a team of 4 friends and I had 2 days to complete a game following the theme of "mask". We decided to make a 2D platforming puzzle game called "Slime Escape". In this game, you play as a slime that steals masks from enemies and uses them to pretend to be on their team. This simple concept expanded to 13 unique levels, 10 standard and 3 challenge (unusual or very difficult) levels. Although the judges didn't pick our game, I am still very proud of what we created, so even after the game jam, we continued to update our game.
